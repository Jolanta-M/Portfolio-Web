The gap in existing mobile decision applications

The review of existing mobile decision applications in the light of relevant psychological research showed that most apps are providing only the analytical decision tools such as Pros & Cons, Eisenhower Matrix etc., but almost none are providing the tools to master the intuitive decision style, otherwise known as ‘gut feeling’.

The key psychological research on human decisions which influenced this project

1. Dijksterhuis et al. (2006) - proves that conscious decisions do not always help to reach a satisfactory choice and that often in more complex decisions the intuition, which is close to unconscious thinking, leads to better outcomes. One or the other decision style is important depending on the situation.

2. Research of G. Gigerenzer - claims that heuristic approach underlies the human ‘gut feeling’. When people act based on the ‘rule of thumb’ or heuristics, they act rationally. In complex decisions heuristic approach produces better outcomes.

3. Research of Wang et al. (2015) - reviews the opposing schools of thought on intuition vs. analysis. Researchers conclude that rationality and intuition are independent of each other and happen simultaneously.

1. Dijksterhuis, A. et al. (2006) 'On making the right choice: the Deliberation-Without Attention effect,' Science, 311(5763), pp. 1005–1007. Available at: https://doi.org/10.1126/science.1121629. (Accessed: November 2, 2024).
2. Clavien, C. (2009) 'Gerd Gigerenzer, Gut Feelings: Short cuts to better decision making,' Ethical Theory and Moral Practice, 13(1), pp. 113–115. Available at: https://doi.org/10.1007/s10677-009-9172-8. (Accessed: November 15, 2024).
3. Wang, Y. et al. (2015) 'Meta‐analytic investigations of the relation between intuition and analysis, ' Journal of Behavioral Decision Making, 30(1), pp. 15–25. Available at: https://doi.org/10.1002/bdm.1903. (Accessed: October 27, 2024).

New functionalities as a solution

The new software solution apart from the analytical Pros & Cons tool has additional functionalities:

1. Intuitive Guide with questions

The Intuitive Guide questions used in this tool were written by Megan Livingstone in the article: "5 questions to ask when listening to your gut". Available at: https://www.meghanlivingstone.com/blog. (Accessed: February 5, 2025).

2. REI-40 psychological test to measure the rationality and intuition:

This test was developed by Pacini & Epstein in 1999 and it measures individual thinking styles – rational and intuitive. The thinking styles are measured independently of each other, resulting in two different scores. The user takes this test after signing up to the application.

3. Library of online psychological articles about decision making

Links to the online psychological articles which provide the information about how to make better decisions and discuss difficulties people can have with this.

Usability heuristics of inteface design

The interface design of this decision application is following the ten usability principles of Jacob Nielsen:

1. Visibility of system status

While trying to Sign Up – users get feedback with instructions if their password or email does not match the standards, and a success message if the process of Sign-Up went well. Similar feedback is given when Logging In. Also, the REI-40 test has a count bar for user to know how many questions are still left to answer.

2. Error prevention

Delete buttons / Cancel buttons /Error messages with instructions / Disabled forward or save buttons until the user selects an option

3. Help users recognize, diagnose and recover from errors

Clear and informative Error messages with instructions / Disabled buttons where appropriate / Delete or Cancel buttons

4. Match between the system and the real world

The concept of taking notes and defining our options is known in real life. The functionality of the decision tools reflects this. Sometimes the description of a concept is provided to make it more understandable for the user. Also, in the Pros & Cons tool the user evaluates options and factors using well known Likert scale.

5. User Control and Freedom

In case of a user mistake or change of mind there are buttons available to go Back or return to Home page, to Cancel or Delete.

6. Consistency and Standards

Standard icons were used for Home navigation, User Account navigation. Buttons have a standard naming, like "Proceed", "Next", "Done". Buttons for going back have the commonly used arrow icons.

7. Recognition rather than recall

To minimize user’s memory load all tools were displayed in a visible way on the home screen. There's also ‘help in context'- the short description of what to do in the context of each step for user to navigate the app without the need to memorise.

8. Aesthetic and minimalist design

Design was made to prioritize the tools and only relevant information in the application.

9. Flexibility and efficiency of use

Personalization was not fully developed because of the time constraints of the project. Future work would involve making an individual interpretation of the REI-40 test results and a welcome greeting by username. Some shortcuts should be added to make the Pros & Cons and Intuitive Guide notes and scores accessible from the home page or the user account. Also, the user background customization could be implemented.

10. Help and documentation

For this decision application no documentation is needed. The main instructions are provided ‘in context’ of each step on the app and in the error messages.

Development of the DecideApp

1. This project was followig Agile Methodology using the guidance of Kniberg (2015). GitHub version control was maintained during the implementation process.

2. Frontend was developed with React Native language and Expo framework. Two versions of the application were built: for Android and for iOS phones. Emulators from Android Studio and Expo Go were used.

3. Backend was developed using JavaScript, Node.js and MongoDB non-relational database.

4. Claude 3.7 Sonnet AI was used as a help for developing some of the components.

Testing and evaluation

This project aims to measure if the perceived usefulness of the mobile decision application with both Pros & Cons and Intuitive Guide tools will be higher than perceived usefulness of application with only Pros & Cons tool.

The research type chosen for this purpose was within-subjects experimental design, where all the participants were exposed to both conditions, in this case - to different versions of the decision application (version with only Pros & Cons tool and new version with additional tools)
