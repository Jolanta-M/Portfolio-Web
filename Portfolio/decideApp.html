The gap in existing mobile decision applications

The review of existing mobile decision applications in the light of relevant psychological research showed that most apps are providing only the analytical decision tools such as Pros & Cons, Eisenhower Matrix etc., but almost none are providing the tools to master the intuitive decision style, otherwise known as ‘gut feeling’.

The key psychological research on human decisions which influenced this project

1. Dijksterhuis et al. (2006) - proves that conscious decisions do not always help to reach a satisfactory choice and that often in more complex decisions the intuition, which is close to unconscious thinking, leads to better outcomes. One or the other decision style is important depending on the situation.

2. Research of G. Gigerenzer - claims that heuristic approach underlies the human ‘gut feeling’. When people act based on the ‘rule of thumb’ or heuristics, they act rationally. In complex decisions heuristic approach produces better outcomes.

3. Research of Wang et al. (2015) - reviews the opposing schools of thought on intuition vs. analysis. Researchers conclude that rationality and intuition are independent of each other and happen simultaneously.

1. Dijksterhuis, A. et al. (2006) 'On making the right choice: the Deliberation-Without Attention effect,' Science, 311(5763), pp. 1005–1007. Available at: https://doi.org/10.1126/science.1121629. (Accessed: November 2, 2024).
2. Clavien, C. (2009) 'Gerd Gigerenzer, Gut Feelings: Short cuts to better decision making,' Ethical Theory and Moral Practice, 13(1), pp. 113–115. Available at: https://doi.org/10.1007/s10677-009-9172-8. (Accessed: November 15, 2024).
3. Wang, Y. et al. (2015) 'Meta‐analytic investigations of the relation between intuition and analysis, ' Journal of Behavioral Decision Making, 30(1), pp. 15–25. Available at: https://doi.org/10.1002/bdm.1903. (Accessed: October 27, 2024).
